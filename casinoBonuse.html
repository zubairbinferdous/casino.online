What is a casino bonus?

The most characteristic feature of any casino in the industry today is online casino bonuses. In case you consider visiting a virtual casino, this aspect will prominently be portrayed somewhere on the site displaying the vast majority of bonuses offered. Therefore, what is a casino bonus? It is an incentive to attract new players to join the casino and maintain the already existing ones by merely rewarding them.

Casino bonuses come in different forms such as additional bonus funds, free bets on casino games (such as free spins on slots), initiatives and gifts such as smartphones on special occasions like birthdays. However, these rewards are earned once a player meets certain conditions which could either be making a minimum deposit, participating in a particular game or referring a friend.

Types of online casino bonuses

Despite luring new players to join a particular casino and keeping the existing ones, casino bonuses make online betting fun to play. Therefore, online casinos have designed different types of bonuses suited for different kinds of players.

Each type of bonus cash varies in terms of the amount offered, financial commitment by the player and requirements required to qualify for the bonus. With so many casino bonuses floating around, it can be very messy and confusing for any new player. For this reason, we have discussed the various casino bonuses in detail to help one understand better.

Welcome bonus

The first casino bonus any new player will come across when looking to register at an online casino is the welcome bonus. First and foremost the casino welcome bonus is designed to attract new casino customers to the casino site. With so much competition in the online casino industry at present, the welcome bonus is the casino's primary chance to lure in new casino customers.

Just about all casino operators will have a welcome bonus of some form, it could be a bonus funds reward or deposit bonus free spins to play on the casino's online slot machines and games. The welcome bonus is one of the best casino bonuses which can represent good value for money for new casino players to take advantage of.

No deposit bonus

The no-deposit bonus has become very popular in recent times. The unique feature of the specific bonus is that players do not have to deposit any amount to win it. Although the amount is usually small or a few free spins, players essentially get bonus funds to try the casino games with (or deposit the bonus funds to contribute the free spins to check out the slots).

A no-deposit bonus mainly targets new casino customers joining an online casino. It offers them a chance to try out new casino games without depositing money. However, it is essential to note that the wagering requirement for this type of bonus is usually very high. This includes making a qualifying deposit before cashing out on any bonus winnings made as a result of the free bet.

Additionally, apart from the wagering requirements, there are game restrictions for a no-deposit casino bonus. It is highly unlikely to find the no-deposit bonuses attached to blackjack or poker games.

The non-sticky bonuses have also become very popular in recent years. It lowers risks with no need to wager, yet the promise of high rewards is very encouraging.

First deposit bonus

The first deposit bonus is a primary component of the casino sign-up bonus for new players signing up for the first time at an online casino. By making an initial minimum deposit to a player’s account, the operator will reward you with a casino bonus usually given as a percentage match and the maximum amount offered. Unlike the no-deposit bonuses, the deposit bonus offers players a relatively high amount.

For instance, an online casino offers a 50% bonus of up to £100. To claim this bonus a player decides to make a first minimum deposit worth £100 to his account. The operator will credit his account with an additional £50 bringing the total amount to £150. In case the player deposited, say £150, the player would only earn £100 since the maximum limit on the bonus is £100.

Besides the percentage deposit bonus, an additional reward accompanies the deal such as bonus spins on a particular game. Players must bear in mind the wagering requirements must be met before enjoying this bonus.

During regular casino play, you can encounter the following types of First Deposit Bonuses:

  1. 100% Match Deposit Bonus: Commonly referred to as a match deposit bonus, it is the most typical first deposit bonus offered to new casino customers in most online casinos. This means, that for any amount deposited, you will receive a deposit match based on the maximum offer.
  2. 200% Match Deposit Bonus: Although less common than the 100% deposit match bonus, the 200% bonus is generally a good offer that may come as a second or third deposit bonus. Any online casino with such an offer means it pays twice the amount deposited by a player. In other words, it’s twice what a match bonus would give you.
  3. 300% Match Deposit Bonus: This type of bonus is quite rare to come across in the UK. In case an online casino decides to run it, very high wagering requirements usually accompany it. This means that gamblers get thrice the amount of money they deposit, which would significantly boost their bankroll.
  4. 500% Match Deposit Bonus: If you thought 300% was the highest online bonus, you are wrong! The 500% bonus is the highest offer yet among online casinos, however, extremely rare. In most cases, it is often reserved for high rollers who deposit vast sums of cash into the casino in one go.

Different bonus conditions to consider

As enticing as they may appear, online casino bonuses have different conditions and restrictions players need to abide by in order to withdraw the bonus. A typical condition is the playthrough requirements. It refers to the number of times you have to wager the bonus before withdrawing any money. In most cases, it ranges between 10 and 50 times.

Players need to ensure that they have placed cash stakes on casino games that count towards the wagering requirements; otherwise, the bonus will be cancelled. Additionally, some operators only allow specific banking methods to be used for deposits. Otherwise, any withdrawals are forfeited. Remember – when in doubt, read the terms and conditions thoroughly!

Bonus limits

Online gambling sites are cautious about the number of bonuses they offer. Thus bonus limits are set; otherwise, there would be no casinos in operation. When it comes to deposit bonuses, it is not possible to get an infinite amount of money as most players might think.

Most gamblers focus on the percentage part without reading the rest of the bonus statement. Regardless of the amount of money the player deposits, a casino bonus will only offer a certain amount (up to the maximu bonus amount).

Take, for instance, a welcome bonus that offers a 100% match up to £250. If a punter deposits £100 the casino will credit his account with £100 extra. However, in case a deposit of £200 is made, the casino will credit the player's account with £250.

The max bonus conversion to real funds also needs to be taken into account as it is often limited to a certain amount as well.

Wagering requirements

As previously mentioned, most casino bonuses need to be wagered a certain number of times before any winnings from the bonus can be withdrawn. Although a majority of players view this as a way to deny them the bonus, it is beneficial to the operator. This strategy protects the virtual casino from fraudsters whose aim is to acquire the bonus and leave without making any payments.

The wagering requirement is usually a multiplier with the highest reaching up to 50x. The higher the wagering requirements, the more difficult it is to withdraw the bonus. For example, if a player earns £50 as a bonus with a 20x wagering requirement means that he will have to stake £1000 on select casino games before cashing out any proceeds.

Remember that bonus funds are separate from cash funds, and sometimes only bonus funds count towards the wagering requirement. It is imperative to note that some online gambling sites will only allow a player to withdraw a portion of the bonus money even after the wagering requirements have been met. In such cases, the full bonus amount cannot be withdrawn.

Wagering contributions

Wagering contributions vary by game and that is one of the most overlooked aspects of casino bonuses. Put simply, different games contribute differently to the wagering requirements. Often expressed as a percentage, it indicates how wagers on particular games count towards completing the wagering requirements.

In most cases, depending on a particular online casino, chance-based casino games like slots and lottery tickets might contribute 100%. On the other hand, skill-based games such as blackjack might only count for 25% of the wagering contribution. Generally, casino games that have a higher RTP (return to player) have a smaller contribution percentage.

For this reason, wagering your bonus on some popular slots such as Starburst will complete the wagering contribution faster than betting on live dealer games or table games. The contribution percentage varies from one operator to another. Therefore, players should do a thorough investigation before making any commitments.

What are wagering requirements?

Wagering requirements are the total you should bet/wager to discharge your extra rewards into your Main Balance. With most bonus funds or free spins, you won’t have the capacity to make withdrawals at all until the point that you have finished wagering the entire bonus funds the required amount of times.

For example, on the off chance that you have £10 bonus funds with 50x prerequisites, you should stake an aggregate of £500 so that the first bonus funds expire and will go towards your main balance. This will then allow you to withdraw those bonus funds just like you would any other winnings.
