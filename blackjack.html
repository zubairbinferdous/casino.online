Additional Rules

Splitting Pairs

Splitting pairs happens when a gamer’s initial two-card hand includes two cards of the same value. The player can split the card into two separate hands when it is their turn and then receive an extra card for each hand from the dealer.

Double Down

Doubling down when playing blackjack online happens when a player doubles their starting bet in the middle of a hand after which they receive an extra card. There are three situations in which doubling down is profitable; your cards total 11 and the dealer’s face-up is lower, you have a soft 16, 17, or 18, and when you have a hard nine, the dealer’s face-up card is two-six.

Insurance

Insurance acts as a side bet separate from the original stake when playing blackjack online. It is offered when the dealer’s up-card is an ace and is insured against the dealer’s hand ‘blackjack’. An insurance bet pays out at 2/1 with the maximum bet being half a player’s original wager.

Settlement

In settling a bet, the dealer determines the result of the bet whether it is a winner or a loser. When a winning bet is settled, a gamer is able to claim his/her winnings. Winning wagers happen when the dealer does not have a Blackjack, has a count value of more than 21 or less than the player’s hand, or has a Blackjack in an insurance wager.

Blackjack Basic Strategy - How to Win

Winning in blackjack requires a player to play each hand in an optimum way and this takes into account the dealer's upcard. When the dealer has a good upcard - a 7, 8, 9, 10-card or ace - the gamer should not stop drawing until a total of 17 or more is attained. When the upcard is a poor one - 4, 5, or 6 - the player should stop drawing after getting a total of 12 or higher. The aim is to never take a card if there is a chance of busting. Another blackjack strategy worth mentioning is that when the dealer’s upcard is a fair one - 2 or 3 - the gamer should stop with a total of 13 or more.

The basic strategy for a soft hand is to keep hitting until a value of at least 18 is reached. For doubling down, the blackjack strategy is to do so with a total of 11. If the value is 10, the player should double down unless the dealer shows an ace or a 10-card. For a value of 9, the gamer should double down if the dealer has fair or poor cards.

5 Mistakes New Blackjack Players Do

Live Blackjack Variants

There are dozens of live blackjack variants where the rules and the gameplay change a bet. Here are a few of the most popular variations:

Free Bet Blackjack

In this blackjack game, the player can double down or split wagers for free after making their initial wager. The gamer, therefore, gets two very rewarding side bets in addition to the regular side bets placed.

Power Blackjack

This variant of blackjack allows a player to power double - to split any two cards (they don’t have to be the same). A player can also power split meaning they can exchange the double-down card for the next card in the shoe.

 Speed Blackjack

As the name suggests, speed blackjack is a fast-paced version of the blackjack game. In this seven-seat game, the dealer does not go from left to right but rather cards are dealt to the fastest players first. After the first two cards are dealt, the gamer who makes the quickest decision to split/hit/double down gets the next card.

Blackjack Party

Blackjack Party is similar to the standard Live Blackjack only that it has a party-infused theme making it quite special. The presentation of this blackjack game is unique as the dealers make it their goal to entertain players.

Infinite Blackjack

This variant is great for gamers looking to play blackjack with low bets. Infinite blackjack is a low-wager version of blackjack that can be played with an infinite number of players. Gamers are dealt the same hand at the beginning of each round after which they can make their decision.

History of Blackjack

While the history of Twenty-One dates from World War I, its roots can be traced to the late 1760s in France. Other records of the game occurred in Britain during the 1770s and 1780s under the name Vingt-Un. Still known then as Vingt-Un, blackjack was first introduced to the United States in the early 1800s during the First World War. English Vingt-Un then developed into an American version in its own right which received the name blackjack around 1899.

A popular myth has it that when Vingt-Un was introduced into the United States, gaming spots offered bonus payouts to provide a remarkable gaming experience. One bonus was a 10:1 payout if a gamer had included the ace of spades and a blackjack. This hand was popularly known as a “blackjack” and the name remained even after the Blackjack bonuses was withdrawn.
