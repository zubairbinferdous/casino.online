Top 3 Casino Games with the Best Odds

The online casino, much like its regular counterpart, is all about beating the odds and getting that edge which will help not only bonus funds but you beat the house. After all, while the best casino games and all bonus wagering requirement and certain amount of skill involved, that doesn’t mean that the main deciding factor in deciding whether you win or lose is chance.

You can be an expert at knowing when to hit or fold in blackjack, but even that won’t save you from bad hands which lure you in with low numbers and then slap you with a King the moment you reach 12! While it’s true that the house always wins at the end, which online real money casino games will give you the best fighting chance, should luck be on your side?

Blackjack

A classic of casino table games and online table games. It’s generally very difficult to tell the exact house edge of blackjack due to the fact that most casinos use multiple decks. Sure, with one deck your odds of winning would be about 70%, but that would be different at a casino using 3 decks, or 6, or 8.

With that said, it's best to remember that each combination of cards you and your dealer receive come with their own odds of success. For example, getting a 12 while your dealer gets a 10 means that it’s very likely your dealer will win this hand, as they won’t go bust no matter what they draw, and the most likely scenario is that they’ll end up with 20 (as there’s way more cards worth 10 points in the deck than any other).

So, your best bet is to hit and hope for an 8 or 9 - what have you got to lose? Meanwhile, if you have a 12, but your dealer has a mid-range card, for example 5, it’s better to stand. You’re not in a great position, but you’re way more likely to draw a 10 and bust, so your best bet is to hope the dealer draws another low card and then busts.

All of these rules and probabilities make blackjack games, especially live blackjack, a very complicated game to play - but also a crazy satisfying one when you get it right.

Roulette

After the complicated and often confusing odds of blackjack, it’s almost refreshing to talk about roulette, where the odds are simple, straightforward and universal UK online casino experience. That’s because, unlike blackjack, roulette is entirely a game of chance. The odds for any number in the game of roulette winning is almost always 1 to 37, or about 2.7%.

While there’s only 36 numbers you can bet on in typical roulette games, there’s also a 0 which serves as a “house edge” - meaning that if the ball lands on 0 the casino wins. Your odds of winning drop down to 2.6% when playing American roulette, which doubles the house edge by featuring a 00 in addition to the regular 0.

Of course, just because the odds are simple doesn't mean there aren't ways to slightly tilt them in your favour! If you still want to bet on numbers, then your best chance is to play French roulette, which doesn't alter the odds from regular roulette, but will give you half your bet back if the ball falls on 0. In addition, you could always bet on the group or color of the number instead of on the actual number, which will significantly increase your odds of winning!

Betting on black or red will give you a 48.6% chance of winning (because they had to make that pesky zero a green), and the same holds true for betting on odd or even. Some casinos will also let you bet on 1-18 or 19-36, and, you guessed it, te odds are exactly the same - but naturally, winning a bet like that will also give you a substantially lower win than betting on a certain number. If you want to increase your win you could always try betting on a group of 12 numbers, which will give you odds of 12/37, or 32%.

Baccarat

Many may know baccarat only as the game which James Bond plays in the original novel Casino Royale (which was changed to poker for the movie adaptation), but the fact remains that it's one of the best live casino games available at online casinos - and its enduring popularity proves it. The rules are fairly simple, with the highest bidding player (the Player) and the live dealer games (the Banker) being dealt two cards.

All players can choose on whether they want to bet on the Player, the Banker or on a tie. After that whoever makes it the closest to 9 without going over wins, and the bet is paid one to one... Sort of.

In a baccarat game, the house edge comes from the fact that the banker has slightly better odds of winning due to being allowed to draw a third card. Since bettors can bet on either the player or the banker, this makes the banker the option with the obvious advantage - and that's where the house edge kicks in, as the casino will take 5% of bets placed on the banker. Sneaky, yet clever!

How do casino games work?

Table games and online slot machine games rely on what's known as a random numbers generator, a computer algorithm which will pull up a random number in order to, say, determine how the reels will line up on a slot, or which card will be drawn, or where the ball will land on the roulette wheel. These generators have been thoroughly tested to ensure that they're truly random, so there's no risk of fraud when it comes to the games. If you're still uncertain you can always play live casino where all the props are physical, and as such there's no way for the computer to cheat.

What casino games have the best odds of winning?

Undeniably blackjack is the leader here - while counting cards is not very practical even at UK online casinos (due to the decks being shuffled after every deal), if you take a couple of hours to research when to bet and when not to your chances of winning will go above 50%, and that's by far the best you can hope for in a casino!

Where can I find the biggest jackpots?

Jackpots are usually tied to specific slots, such as Mega Moolah - and some smaller, newer online casinos in the UK won't typically host these types of games because they wouldn't be able to pay out the jackpot if someone won it. Try to type jackpot into the casino's games search bar and see if you can find one of the progressive jackpot slots with the best odds of winning. If there aren't any jackpot slots, and you really want to play for those sweet bundles of cash, it might be a good idea to try another casino.

Which games are best for beginners?

This depends entirely on what you're looking for out of online casinos. Do you want to play games and maybe earn a bit of real money on the side, or are you solely enticed by the monetary reward? If it's the former, starting with a skill-based game like blackjack or baccarat is a good way to stay invested. If you're just looking for wins, then slots might be your best bet - sure, the odds of winning the big bucks are fairly low, but it requires no effort or skill at all. If you're somewhere in the middle, roulette is the best of both worlds!

Well, it might sound like the obvious advice, but learning how to play the more skill-based casino games is the only way to improve your odds of winning.
