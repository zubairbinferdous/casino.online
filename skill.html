History of Skrill - How Skrill Came to Be

Skrill was established in 2001 by Benjamin Kullmann and Daniel Klein. The service was created as an internet-focused payment service. It was originally called Moneybookers, a name that you are most likely to find in some older casinos. It is now a full-fledged digital wallet that offers a wide number of services that include storing cash, making automatic payments, receiving and sending cash, and paying subscriptions.

In the recent past, the company has changed hands several times. It was first purchased by InvestCorp Technology in 2007 and then by PaySafe in 2015. The company then sold Skrill to a consortium of private equity investors late in the year 2017. Today, the company is led by Lorenzo Pellegrino with the support of Joel Leonoff, PaySafe CEO. Skrill has expanded its service to cover integrations with social media platforms and digital eCommerce sites.
